WHAT NOW?

9th Graders:
  • Focus primarily on keeping grades up
  • Begin an extracurricular activity you’re passionate about
  • Try to start a project surrounding a community need
  • Begin journaling all your meaningful life experiencesg

10th Graders:
  • Within an extracurricular activity, try to show initiative by starting a new project
  • Try to ensure that your grades are equal to or better than last year’s
  • Keep up with a journal if you’ve started one, or start one if you haven’t
  • Start thinking about what you want in a college experience in general terms

11th Graders:
  • It’s your most important year in terms of academic performance, so study up for first semester finals
  • If your performance is slipping in a specific class, make sure to meet with your teacher outside of class to help you improve
  • Start thinking about a college list and speak with your high school counselor to help
  • If you have a few specific schools on your list already, register for a virtual tour and connect with a local admissions representative
  • Begin a study regimen for Spring SAT and/or ACT tests
  • Make sure to remain active in at least a couple of your extracurricular activities

12th Graders:
  • Keep your fingers crossed for those early applications!
  • Be sure to create accounts for and log into your school portals at least a couple times each week once you’ve applied
  • Don’t let your first semester grades slip
  • Plan out your supplemental essays for Regular Decision applications
  • Make sure to send quick thank you notes to each member of your schools’ admissions teams if they help you with anything

SCHOOL SPOTLIGHT: INDIANA UNIVERSITY

Indiana University

Indiana University is a large, public research university with its state flagship campus in Bloomington, Indiana. The Bloomington campus alone enrolls over 40,000 students in over 1,000 different academic programs. The IU system is known best for its Business and Nursing programs, robust greek life, and of course, a storied Basketball program.

IU’s Kelley School of Business is highly ranked for a reason, and 10 of its 18 business-oriented majors are considered to be among the top 20 in their fields nationwide. Many Kelley students apply for direct admission, which requires submitting their applications by November 1, meeting test score requirements of 30+ (ACT) or 1370 (SAT), and maintaining a weighted GPA of 3.8+. Test-optional applicants to Kelley must petition the school directly to obtain direct admission to its Business programs. Undeclared students are invited to transfer into Kelley after their first year if they have maintained B grades in all IU courses and met specific coursework completion requirements.
